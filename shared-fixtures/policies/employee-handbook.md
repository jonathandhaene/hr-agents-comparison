# Zava Employee Handbook (excerpt)

> This is sample content for demo purposes only. It is intentionally brief and does not represent a complete employee handbook.

## 1. Welcome to Zava

Zava is a specialty coffee retailer and supplier headquartered in Seattle, Washington. We employ approximately 5,000 people across 12 countries. This handbook summarizes the key policies that apply to all employees regardless of location, with country-specific addenda where applicable.

## 2. Working at Zava

### 2.1 Work model

Zava operates a **hybrid work model**. Employees are expected to be on-site at their assigned office a minimum of **two (2) days per week**, unless their role is designated fully remote.

### 2.2 Working hours

Standard working hours are **40 hours per week**, generally Monday through Friday. Local norms apply where they differ. Flexible scheduling is supported with manager approval.

### 2.3 Code of conduct

All Zava employees are required to complete annual **Code of Conduct training** within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter. Failure to complete required training may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## 3. Compensation & benefits

### 3.1 Pay schedule

Most employees are paid **bi-weekly** (every other Friday). Some countries follow a monthly pay cycle per local norms. See your country addendum.

### 3.2 Benefits enrollment

Newly hired employees have **30 days from start date** to enroll in benefits. Annual open enrollment runs each November. Outside these windows, qualifying life events (marriage, birth, adoption, loss of other coverage) trigger a 30-day special enrollment window.

### 3.3 Retirement (US)

US employees are eligible for the Zava 401(k) plan starting on day 1. Zava matches **100% of the first 6%** of eligible compensation. Vesting is immediate.

## 4. Time off

See the dedicated [PTO Policy](./pto-policy.md).

## 5. Performance & growth

### 5.1 Performance cycles

Zava runs **two formal review cycles per year** (H1 review in June, H2 review in December). Each cycle includes a self-assessment, peer feedback (360°), and a manager review.

### 5.2 Internal mobility

Employees in good standing with **at least 12 months of tenure** in their current role are eligible to apply for internal openings. Notify your current manager before submitting an internal application.

## 6. Reporting concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that conflicts with our values or the law, you have multiple ways to raise it:

- Talk to your manager.
- Talk to your HR Business Partner (see the HR directory in the company portal).
- Use the anonymous **Speak-Up** hotline.

Retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith is strictly prohibited.

## 7. Data privacy & information security

Employees must complete annual **Information Security** and **Privacy Foundations** training. Data classification, acceptable use, and incident-reporting expectations are detailed in the Information Security Policy on the company portal.
